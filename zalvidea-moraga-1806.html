Expeditions of Zalvidea & Moraga, 1806–1807

Route Reconstructions from S. F. Cook

Zalvidea (Jul–Aug 1806): Santa Ynez Mission through the Cuyama Valley to Buena Vista Lake and the Kern country; returned via Tejon Pass to San Gabriel Mission.
Moraga (Sep–Oct 1806): San Juan Bautista east through Pacheco Pass; north up the San Joaquin Valley to the Cosumnes River; south along the Sierra foothills through the Kings, Kaweah, Tule, and Kern rivers; exited via Grapevine Canyon to San Fernando Mission.
S. F. Cook, Colonial Expeditions to the Interior of California Central Valley, 1800–1820, Ch. III (pp. 245–255), endnotes pp. 136–142. Diary by Fr. Pedro Muñoz (Moraga) and Fr. José María de Zalvidea.
