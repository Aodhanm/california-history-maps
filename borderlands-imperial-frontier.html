Bay Area Borderlands

San Francisco Bay as Imperial Frontier, 1775–1841

75+ events mapping the convergence of four empires at San Francisco Bay. Every actor — Spanish viceroys, British navigators, Russian fur traders, American merchants, and the indigenous peoples who controlled the north shore — recognized that effective Spanish power stopped at the Bay.

Spanish expeditions north of SF were always round trips — nobody stayed.
Russian outposts (Ross, Bodega, three ranchos) were permanent.
British & American vessels tested the defenses and found nothing.
Indigenous peoples — Coast Miwok, Suisun, Kashaya Pomo — chose sides, brokered intelligence, and controlled the territory between the empires.

The convergence zone: Bodega–Petaluma–Sonoma triangle, where Russian and Spanish/Mexican spheres met.
Sources: C-A manuscripts (Bancroft Library / Internet Archive); Menzies 1924; Vancouver 1798; Farris 1998; Milliken 2009; Gibson & Istomin 2014; AVPRI (Fort Ross Conservancy); Bancroft, History of California I–V; Wagner 1931.
