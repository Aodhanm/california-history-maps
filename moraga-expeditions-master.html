Gabriel Moraga — All Known Expeditions, 1806–1817

Route Reconstructions from Cook, Bancroft, Langelier & Rosen

1806: Central Valley. SJB → Pacheco Pass → Cosumnes → Kern → Tejon → San Fernando. 43 days.
1807: Tulares. Monterey → San Miguel → Buena Vista Lake → mountains.
1810 May: Suisun Campaign. SF Presidio → Carquinez crossing → Battle at Malaca's ranchería. 17 men vs. ~120 Suisun.
1810 Aug: Delta. San José → Antioch → Delta → Gustine → SJB.
1810 Oct: Delta (punitive). San José → Bolbones → Merced → Santa Clara.
1810 Oct: Bodega Recon. SF → Marin → Bodega Bay. 10-day reconnaissance. Found American ship, Aleut hunters.
1812: Fort Ross. SF → Ross. Identified 150-yard stockade + cannon.
1813–14: Fort Ross. Two trips. Drove cattle as diplomacy; carried demands for Russian withdrawal.
1817: San Rafael. SF → Marin. Selected site for first establishment north of SF Bay.
Cook, Colonial Expeditions (1960); Bancroft, History of California, Vol. II; Langelier & Rosen, Historic Resource Study: Presidio of SF (1992); Vallejo, Recuerdos, i. 131–5.
